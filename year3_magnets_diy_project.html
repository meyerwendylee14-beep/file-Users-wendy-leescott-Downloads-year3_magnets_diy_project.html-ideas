Safety Rules (Must Follow)

  • Never put magnets near your mouth or nose. Small magnets can be dangerous if swallowed.
  • Keep magnets away from phones, tablets, computers, and credit cards.
  • Use tape/glue carefully. Ask an adult/teacher for help with scissors or hot glue.
  • Make safe toys and games. No “weapons” or anything that can hurt someone.
  • Clean up small pieces so nobody steps on them.

Teacher note: If you choose slime, it must be teacher-approved with adult help.

Project Steps

  1. Choose your idea (toy or game). What will it do?
  2. Plan: draw a quick sketch and list materials.
  3. Build a first version (prototype).
  4. Test: does it work? What needs fixing?
  5. Improve: change one thing to make it better.
  6. Explain the science: Are the magnets attracting (pulling) or repelling (pushing)? Where are the poles?
  7. Share: short demo + 2–3 sentences about how magnets help.

Materials You Can Use

Magnets
  • Small bar/disc magnets
  • Magnetic wand (if available)
  • Paper clips (magnet attracts metal)
Build items
  • Cardboard, paper, cups
  • Bottle caps, straws, string
  • Wooden sticks, LEGO, blocks
Tools
  • Tape, glue, rubber bands
  • Scissors (adult help)
  • Markers for labels
Science words
  • Attract = pull together
  • Repel = push apart
  • Poles = N and S ends
